Baobab Gambit

Baobab Gambit is a dusk-lit tactics duel inspired by the Madagascan game Fanorona. Every turn asks the same sharp question: do you approach the enemy line or withdraw from it to capture instead? Chain those captures cleanly, avoid repeating a lane, and trap the AI before the board locks into a dry-season standoff.

How To Play

  • If any capture exists, you must take a capture instead of a quiet move.
  • Approach: step toward an enemy line so the next point beyond your landing spot holds opponents.
  • Withdrawal: step away from an adjacent enemy line so the point behind your starting spot holds opponents.
  • During a capture chain, the same stone may continue, but it cannot revisit a point or use the same direction twice in a row.
  • If 12 full turns pass without a capture, the side with more stones wins the dry-season standoff.

Controls

Desktop: click a stone, then a glowing destination. Arrow keys move focus around the board, Enter or Space activates a point, A chooses approach, W chooses withdrawal, E ends a capture chain, N starts a fresh match, and M toggles sound.

Why This One

The existing arcade favors real-time play. This entry deliberately shifts into board tactics: no physics flicks, no racing line, no shmup stream. Its identity is the unusual approach-versus-withdraw capture rule and the optional chain that follows.

Original code, presentation, AI, and audio. Inspired by published descriptions of the Malagasy strategy game Fanorona and adapted here as a polished 5x5 browser duel.
